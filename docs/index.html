cancerstudio

Two DNA samples in.
One mRNA vaccine out.

A desktop studio for designing personalized mRNA cancer vaccines. Give it tumor DNA and matched-normal DNA. The pipeline finds what mutated, predicts what the immune system can target, and designs a vaccine construct. For humans, dogs, and cats — all on your local machine.

See the pipeline GitHub

Start

Pick a species. Name your workspace.

Every case is a workspace. Human, dog, or cat — cancerstudio loads the right reference genome and locks the pipeline to that species for the rest of the run.

Ingest

Tumor and matched normal. Paired-end or nothing.

Two lanes: the tumor biopsy and a matched normal reference. cancerstudio reads the files in place, validates that both lanes are paired-end, previews a few reads, and normalizes everything into canonical FASTQ before alignment unlocks.

Align

strobealign against the species reference.

Pick whole-genome or whole-exome and hit start. Reference bundles are fetched and indexed on first run; subsequent runs reuse them. The output is sorted, indexed BAMs with QC metrics ready for variant calling.

Stays on your laptop

Desktop-first, by design.

Electron shell, local Next.js renderer, local FastAPI engine, SQLite on disk. No cloud services, no Docker, no object storage. Your sequencing data never leaves the machine.

Inspired by Paul Conyngham's personalized mRNA vaccine for his dog Rosie.
